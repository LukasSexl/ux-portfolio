01At-a-Glance
The course being called Bespoke Design, the main idea was to tailor the design specifically according to the needs of an individual user, We were assigned a classmate we should get to know and perform research on, so to lay the foundation for designing a fitting gift giving experience.
02Task
I was assigned Vy, a female colleague from Vietnam, and through various research methods I got to know her on a deep, personal level. I needed to collect data and define insights on how to design a fitting gift giving experience tailored to her needs specifically.
03Data Gathering
First we met in a café to get to know each other and get familiar. Alone from that meeting I could get a rough picture of her personality.

Afterwards I formulated 3 main research questions that should guide my research to achieve the goal:

How does the user show appreciation and acknowledge affection from the people in their social circle?
What cultural norms or values did the users' background and upbringing emphasise on regarding gift-giving practises?
Which activities and preferences contribute to the users' well-being?


First Interview

I decided on a topic list and created probes for the first interview:
empathy mapping (say, think, feel, do)
associative activity sheet
visual storytelling
that should trigger the user’s experiences that I would ask follow up questions about.


Second Interview

I created an IRL diary study probe.
Here the user gets a few prompts each day that they need to answer in a diary. This would allow for real life data. Then I would interview Vy on the things she wrote.

06What I learned
I learned to apply various research methods, analyse data and to work with a real user. Not only a language barrier, but also creating a comfortable atmosphere for the interviews were some challenges I faced. I managed to become good friends with Vy and generally I enjoyed the whole process.
